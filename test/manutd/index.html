    Ferguson years

    Alex Ferguson and his assistant Archie Knox arrived from Aberdeen on the day of Atkinson's dismissal, and guided the club to an 11th-place finish in the league. Despite a second-place finish in 1987–88, the club was back in 11th place the following season. Reportedly on the verge of being dismissed, victory over Crystal Palace in the 1990 FA Cup Final replay (after a 3–3 draw) saved Ferguson's career.

The club holds the record for most top-division titles – including a record 13 Premier League titles – FA Cups and FA Community Shields. It was also the first English club to win the European Cup in 1968, and, as of 2016, is the only British club to have won the Club World Cup, in 2008. United also became the sole British club to win the Intercontinental Cup in 1999. The club's most recent trophy came in February 2017, with the 2016–17 EFL Cup.
